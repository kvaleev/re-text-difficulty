## **1. Overview**

Following the complete or major loss of system generation (blackout), it will be
necessary to establish initial generation that can supply a source of electric power to
other system generation and begin system restoration. These initiating generators are
referred to as system Black Start generators. They must be able to self-start without
any source of off-site electric power and maintain adequate voltage and frequency
while energizing isolated transmission facilities and auxiliary loads of other
generators. Generators that can safely reject load down to their auxiliary load are
another form of Black Start generator that can aid system restoration, but are not
addressed with this Black Start Capability Plan (BCP).

From a planning perspective, a system Black Start Capability Plan (BCP) is necessary
to ensure that the quantity and location of system Black Start generators are sufficient
and that they can perform their expected functions as specified in overall coordinated
WECC BCP.

The CAISO may from time to time test Unit(s) designated to provide Black Start
service (through an RMR or Interim Black Start Contract) by requiring the Unit to
deliver Black Start service pursuant to a Test Dispatch Notice provided to Owner’s
Scheduling Coordinator using the procedures described in the Tariff Sections 4.2 and
4.3. Such Test Dispatch Notice shall be marked “Black Start Test Notice.” The Black
Start Test shall be performed in accordance with the Ancillary Services Requirements
Protocol in the CAISO Tariff. The CAISO shall not request a Black Start Test for a
hydroelectric Unit during periods of constrained water availability.

## **2. Grid Planning**

The following provides general guidance for CAISO Black Start planning:

|Step|Description|
|---|---|
|1|The CAISO determines the amount and location of Black Start Generation<br>it requires through contingency studies that are used as the basis of the<br>CAISO’s emergency plans. The studies specify:<br>• The initiating disturbance,<br>• The magnitude of the Outage, including the extent of the Outage (local<br>area, CAISO Controlled Grid, or WECC),<br>• The assumed status of Generation after the initiating disturbance,<br>• The status of interconnections,<br>• The system Demand level at the time of the disturbance,<br>• The interconnection support, and assumptions regarding the availability<br>of support from other utilities to help restore Generation and Demand<br>• The generator performance including a percentage of Black Start units<br>(to be determined by the CAISO) which are expected to fail to start,<br>• Expected transmission system damage.|
|2|The CAISO annually verifies that the number, size, availability, and<br>location of system Black Start generating units are sufficient to meet<br>WECC restoration plan requirements for the CAISO Balancing Authority<br>Area.|
|3|The CAISO documents the Cranking Paths, including initial switching<br>requirements, between each Black Start generating unit and the units to be<br>started and provides this documentation for review to WECC upon<br>request. Such documentation may include Cranking Path diagrams.|
|4|The CAISO Black Start planning evaluation process includes the<br>following:<br>• Diverse fuel resources for Black Start power for generating units, <br>• Available cranking and transmission paths, <br>• Communication adequacy, <br>• Unit capability of maintaining adequate regulation of voltage and<br>frequency, <br>• Generator protection and control systems during the abnormal<br>conditions that will exist during system restoration, <br>• Limited energy resources (e.g., hydro, pumped storage hydro,<br>compressed air) are selected sparingly.|
|5|The planning evaluation process is coordinated, as appropriate, with the<br>Black Start capability plans of transmission owners and neighboring<br>Balancing Authorities.|
|6|The CAISO annually enters into RMR and Interim Black Start Contracts<br>with generators that satisfy the results of the annual planning evaluation<br>process.|

## **6. Definitions**










|Term|Definition|
|---|---|
|**Availability**<br>**Test**|A test called by the CAISO or the RMR Unit Owner to establish<br>and verify the RMR Unit’s Availability Limit.|
|**Black Start**|The ability of a generating unit or station to go from a shutdown<br>condition to an operating condition and start delivering energy to<br>the grid without initial assistance from the electric system_._|
|**Interim**<br>**Black Start**<br>**Unit**|A generating unit with Black Start capability that enters into an<br>Interim Black Start Contract with the CAISO. This is not an<br>RMR contract.|
|**Participant**|Those generator owners or operators that desire to test their<br>generating units for Black Start capability.|
|**RMR Black**<br>**Start Unit**|A generating unit with Black Start capability that enters into an<br>RMR Black Start Contract with the CAISO.|
|**Voluntary**<br>**Black Start**<br>**Unit**|A generating unit withouta CAISO Black Start contract that<br>desires, for whatever reason, to be recognized as a CAISO Black<br>Start generating unit.|
